modelun

A harness for running the same stimulus across many models and reading the columns. The studies below are one investigation that kept refining its instrument: it started by asking how models behave, noticed they behave alike, and ended up measuring exactly how much — and which ones don't.

The arc: conduct put models under multi-turn pressure and found their conduct converging into a few house styles. convergence chased that on open-ended prompts and hit confound after confound — verbosity, sampling temperature, embeddings that conflate style with topic. surprisal is where it landed: constrain answers to one word, and the convergence — and the rare models that escape it — measures cleanly.

where it landed

surprisal — which models give answers the field doesn't?

39 models × 30 one-word prompts ("Name a cheese"). A per-model uniqueness scorecard — answer-choice surprisal against the field's own distribution — with a models × answers grid and per-model and per-category drill-downs. Mostly the field answers in unison (95% name the same tree); the interesting result is who doesn't.

conduct — a behavioral atlas the start

How language models conduct themselves under pressure: honesty, integrity, attunement across a hard multi-turn conversation. Binary & graded markers, an LLM judge with quote-verification, and a synthesis of house styles — the first hint that distinct labs produce convergent behavior.

convergence — do models give the same answer? the confounds

The open-ended middle chapter: 22 models on prompts like "tell me a joke," measured with embeddings. Every confound we hit — packaging, sampling entropy, verbosity, style-vs-topic entanglement — is documented here, along with the temperature caveat and the null results. The instrument that didn't work, kept for the record.
